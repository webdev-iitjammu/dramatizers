Global Goals of Drama Cub

  • To engage productivity in the collaborative process.
  • To build intellectual and aesthetic understanding of the craft and technique of theatre arts.
  • To develop an appreciation of and respect for the various roles/aspects inherent within the theatrical process
  • To explore the diversity of theatre and its intersection with community, culture and society.
  • To produce diverse productions that challenge students and engage the university community.

Develop specialized skills/training in the theatre arts

Students will work in collaborative/artistic ensembles; specifically enhancing skills of listening/respect towards others. They will possess a broad appreciation for theatre arts and exhibit a fundamental working knowledge of the basic areas of theatre art (acting, directing, design, voice, etc.)

  • Perform a broad spectrum of dramatic material both improvised and scripted, ranging from Realism to non-Realism, classical to contemporary.
  • Develop vocal, physical and imaginative skills to express a broad spectrum of dramatic material.
  • Review, analyze and give constructive criticism on performance.
  • Understand the rehearsal and performance process, including the relationship between the actor and the director, the actor and stage manager, actor and production crew, actor and fellow actors.
  • Understand and apply knowledge of scenic and costume construction techniques, scenic painting methods and lighting and sound application.

Develop knowledge of drama and its literature

There is a history behind everything. One should also understand Design techniques for better performances as well as respect the form of art which is giving him/her chance to express.Students develop ethics, values and responsibilities of a theatre artist

  • Know and operate the lighting and sound equipment for theatrical venues.
  • Understand the evolution of the physical stage and historical staging techniques.
  • Appreciate dramatic works from all parts of the world
  • Clearly illuminate a particular theatrical time period that best represents our current contemporary theatrical view.
  • Devise a plan of action and implement a rehearsal process with student actors.

Script writers, producers,directors and other staff members play equal role while preparing for the act. Drama Club focuses on these skills too so that they couls understand the depth of path they have chosen.

  • Analyze a play from a design perspective, opening the students imagine to “seeing” an environment that expresses the theme and spirit of the play.
  • Analyze a play from a performance perspective, including dramatic structure, character analysis, language investigation, and integrate it within the rehearsal and performance process.
  • Analyze a play from a directorial perspective, paying particular attention to paying particular attention to given circumstances, environmental facts, character analysis, spine & scoring.
  • Understand and apply the responsibilities of theatrical production personnel in the run of a production.
  • Understand fire and safety regulations and the safe operation of back stage shifting of properties, lighting and scenery.

Benefits of the Club

There is a lot of established research about the positive influences from drama, theatre and performing arts, especially on young people.

Self Confidence

Aspects of performing arts, especially improvisation, helps young people to understand how to appraise situations,think outside the box, and be more confident going into unfamiliar situations.

Imagination

Being creative and learning to make creative choices helps students to better at thinking of new ideas, allowing them to view the world around them in new ways.

Empathy

Understanding characters,roles and the subtext of plays and musicals allow students to relate better to different situations, backgrounds, and cultures which encourages them to show compassion and tolerance.

Physical Fitness

Performing, evn most passive performances, requires intensive movement over prolonged period.It improves flexibility, coordination, balance and control.

Communicating Skills

It is obvious that drama,theatre and performing arts improves verbal and nonverbal communication along with the listenig and observation skills.

Emotional Outlet

Acting and Drama allows students to express a range of emotions and encourage them to understand and deal with similar feelings they may be experiencing.

Memory

Rehearsing and performing lines and movements will improve memory.

Concentration

Playing, practicing and performing will develop the ability and skills to focus the mind,the body, and the voice.
